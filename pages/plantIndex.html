PLANT INDEX
PEPEROMIA

Peperomia make great houseplants, with fleshy leaves and stems, are low maintenance, and clean the air. These are great for beginners or as a decorative easy to care for houseplant.

PEPEROMIA OBTUSIFOLIA

Light

Water

Difficulty

Also known as the baby rubber plant, this succulent like peperomia should be watered once or twice a week depending on light levels (water more often the brighter conditions). Similar to the peperomia it is recommended to wait for the soil to dry.

PEPEROMIA SCANDEN

Light

Water

Difficulty

Peperomia Scanden is a great indoor plant, tolerating low light conditions. It is non-toxic so caters to all household conditions. For watering it’s best to wait until the soil is almost completely dry, they don’t like to be overwatered! They grow slowly and can produce a variegated leaf. Overall, a great beginner plant!

PEPEROMIA WATERMELON

Light

Water

Difficulty

Watermelon peperomia is the common name used for a popular species from the Peperomia genus named Peperomia argyreia. Named watermelon for its similarity of appearance to the rind (skin) of a watermelon.These are very easy to grow and can make a good plant choice for the beginner.

FERN

Their love of moist air makes ferns perfect houseplants for bright bathrooms or kitchens. Ferns are low to medium maintenance and require some keeping. Mist your ferns as often as practical, preferably in the morning, keeping them damp and moist. If your fern is underwatered its fonds will turn brown and drop. The plant benefits from bright, indirect light during fall and winter. A location where the plant gets at least two hours of indirect sunlight per day

BOSTON FERN

Light

Water

Difficulty

Boston ferns need bright but indirect sunlight. They aren’t well suited to a spot on a sunny windowsill, instead, keep them out of direct light by placing them on a table in a bright room. They require high humidity and consistently moist soil to thrive. Water this plant regularly, once the top of the soil is starting to dry out.

MAIDENHAIR FERN

Light

Water

Difficulty

The maidenhair are a rewarding plant providing amazing foliage, however require a bit of experimentation. High humidity is key, if low humidity levels be sure to mist often or experiment with house placement, bathrooms can be a great place to keep these plants. Indirect morning sun or afternoon light is ideal, direct light will burn the leaves but too little will cause yellowing and little growth. In terms of watering, keep the soil moist always, but not soggy so a pot with drainage holes is helpful. We suggest watering 2-3 times a week.

PLUMOSA FERN

Light

Water

Difficulty

This plant like the maidenhair, requires high humidity so misting daily is recommended. Keeping the soil evenly moist is also required so watering multiple times a week is generally a good idea. In terms of light, keep it out of bright, direct sunlight. These plants can thrive in shaded areas however can acclimate to light if needed.

SUCCULENTS

Succulents are plants that are adapted to store water in their stem and leaves. These make great indoor plants as they're adapted to survive dry conditions however, need to be cared for carefully to survive long periods. Succulents need moderate indirect sunlight and are only watered once the soil has dried out again.

BURRO'S TAIL

Light

Water

Difficulty

Also known as a Donkey’s Tail, this cool looking cacti requires bright indirect light and well drained soil. This plant can react badly if watered too often, so be wary of this. If taken good care of the burro’s tail will reward you with stunning draped foliage so is definitely worth the investment!

JADE PLANT

Light

Water

Difficulty

The Jade plant is a popular succulent houseplant with fleshy, oval-shaped leaves and thick, woody stems that resemble tiny tree trunks.These are generally undemanding and easy to grow, but they are susceptible to mealy bugs and fungal diseases. Suitable to sunny rooms and windowsills

STRING OF PEARLS

Light

Water

Difficulty

The String of Pearls is a unique houseplant and a slow grower but worth it for an elegant hanging centerpiece. Indirect bright light is key, a shaded area with morning light would be ideal. These plants are very sensitive to overwatering so limit it to once every two weeks during the summer and roughly once a month during winter.
